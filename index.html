Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) are premier engineering institutions in India known for their academic excellence, research contributions, and strong industry connections. While they share the common goal of providing quality education in engineering and technology, there are some differences between individual IITs. These differences can be attributed to factors such as location, age, faculty, facilities, and specializations. The cutoff scores for admissions into different IITs can vary depending on factors such as the popularity of the institute, the number of seats available, and the competition level. Overall, while all IITs maintain a high standard of education and research, these differences make each IIT a unique institution, attracting students with varying interests and aspirations. As a result, students often consider these factors along with their academic and career goals while choosing an IIT for their studies. While the core engineering disciplines are offered in all IITs, some institutes may have stronger expertise and focus in specific areas, such as aerospace engineering, computer science, biotechnology, etc. This can influence the research opportunities available to students.

The Joint Seat Allocation Authority (JoSAA) is responsible for conducting the centralized seat allocation process for admission to various Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs), National Institutes of Technology (NITs), Indian Institutes of Information Technology (IIITs), and other participating institutes. The seat allocation process carried out by JoSAA follows a round system that consists of multiple rounds. Here is a description of the round system in JoSAA. It's important to note that the number of rounds and their specific details may vary each year based on factors like the number of vacant seats, candidate preferences, and the specific guidelines set by JoSAA.
